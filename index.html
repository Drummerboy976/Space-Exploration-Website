The Hubble Telescope

When did it launch?

The Hubble telescope was launched on April 24, 1990. It was launched on the space shuttle named Discovery

How far is it from Earth?

The telescope is located 340 miles above the surface of the earth where it orbits around the planet 15 times per day. It approximately finishes an orbit around the earth every 95 minutes. If you look very closely, you may be able to see the Hubble telescope with binoculars or a telescope as it passes over you.

What has the hubble telescope done?

The Hubble telescope has helped to discover that every major galaxy has a black hole at its center. It also has recently assissted in the discovery of two moons around pluto's orbit. The Hubble telescope is a massive and vital tool that astrologists use to study the universe.


Here is a list of other things that the telecope has discovered:

  • Stellar Populations
  • Dark energy
  • a comet colliding with the planet of Jupiter
